Automatic Environment Shaping
is the Next Frontier in RL

Younghyo Park Gabriel B. Margolis Pulkit Agrawal

Position Paper at ICML 2024

Oral Session: Wed 24 Jul 08:30 AM UTC in [Hall C 1-3]
Full Paper Code Repository

Abstract

Many roboticists dream of presenting a robot with a task in the evening and returning the next morning to find the robot capable of solving the task. What is preventing us from achieving this? Sim-to-real reinforcement learning (RL) has achieved impressive performance on challenging robotics tasks, but requires substantial human effort to set up the task in a way that is amenable to RL. It's our position that algorithmic improvements in policy optimization and other ideas should be guided towards resolving the primary bottleneck of shaping the training environment, i.e., designing observations, actions, rewards and simulation dynamics. Most practitioners don't tune the RL algorithm, but other environment parameters to obtain a desirable controller. We posit that scaling RL to diverse robotic tasks will only be achieved if the community focuses on automating environment shaping procedures.

Key Argument and Findings

The promise of reinforcement learning is to automatically solve decision making problems.

A truly intelligent robot should be able to learn a new task without human supervision, removing the bottleneck to scaling capabilities.

The reality of reinforcement learning is that it requires heavy human supervision to solve tasks of real-world complexity.

Typical stages of using RL to solve a robotics problem. Human effort is offset to environment shaping and behavior evaluation.

Practitioners use standard algorithms but carefully develop task-specific heuristics that masquerade as part of the environment.

Common components of environment shaping in practice.

Separate from the problem of constructing an accurate model, these heuristics are necessary to make optimization feasible.

Without them, prevailing techniques fail.

Common benchmark environments are highly sensitive to shaping, often with numerous local minima in the shaping design.

Environment shaping is an essential tool for RL practitioners; to accelerate progress, RL researchers should focus on developing a framework for understanding how shaping impacts the learning dynamics and reducing its heuristic nature.

Much of the challenge in environment shaping extends beyond reward shaping.
